The sun sinks slow behind the trees
Dust kicks up on old back roads
Windows down and country songs
Playing soft on the radio

Fireflies light up the backyard air
Bare feet running through the grass
Laughing like we’ve got all night
Like we’re praying it never passes

Pickup trucks and faded jeans
Names carved in the water tower paint
Hearts too young to know goodbye
Too wild to ever feel afraid

Maybe someday we’ll grow up and leave
Chasing dreams in bigger towns
But a piece of me will stay right here
Where love is simple and slow goes time
